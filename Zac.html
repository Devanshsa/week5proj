Computational Science

Use of computer science to create models and simulations has paved the way for considerable advancements in science and engineering.

Not to be confused with computer science, computational science is the application of computer science and software engineering principles to solve scientific problems. The simulation of physical processes can tell us a lot about how they'll behave under certain conditions, which is useful for the study or design of physical concepts. Some applications of computational science to engineering are shown below.

MATLAB

MATLAB (Matrix laboratory) is a numerical computing enviroment and programming language.

Originally developed by computer science chairman Cleve Moler for his students as an alternative to using FORTRAN (another language suited to scientific and numerical computation), its potential was recognised by engineer Jack Little. Forming a team and founding MathWorks, they rewrote MATLAB in C and continued its development into the present day.
